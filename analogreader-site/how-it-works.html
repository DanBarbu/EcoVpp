The four-step pipeline

1

Capture

Photo from a phone, tablet, webcam, or ESP32 + camera. JPEG or PNG; we re-encode anything else.

2

Locate & deskew

An on-device YOLOv8n detector finds the meter face, crops it, and corrects perspective. Average runtime: ~80 ms on a modern phone.

3

OCR + confidence

Per-dial digit classifier returns a value plus a confidence score per digit. Anything < 0.80 flags for review.

4

Persist

Reading + image hash + timestamp + DID are stored in your account. Optionally pushed to your utility via the open API.

Confidence scoring

A reading is only as useful as its trustworthiness. Every digit gets its own confidence score from the classifier, and the overall reading is gated at 0.80.

≥ 0.95 auto-accept

Stored directly. No human in the loop. Most clear, well-lit photos land here.

0.80 – 0.95 accept + audit

Stored, but tagged for quick visual confirmation in the dashboard.

< 0.80 review

Routed to a verification queue. You see the photo with the digit boxes and confirm or correct in two taps.

What we read

⚡

Cyclometer dials

The old white-numbers-on-black wheel: 5–6 digits + a red decimal. Average accuracy in our test set: 99.2%.

🔢

LCD digital meters

Seven-segment displays with multi-tariff readouts. We detect the active tariff register from its labels (T1 / T2 / OFF-PEAK).

🔥

Gas dials (m³)

Standard 5-digit m³ dial. Ignores the decimal pointer dials the way utility staff do.

💧

Water dials

m³ + decimal sub-dials. Cold and hot meters separated by photo metadata or manual tag.

Privacy & data residency

Your photos are yours

Stored encrypted at rest, never shared with utilities or third parties without an explicit per-share toggle. You can wipe everything from Settings → Account → Erase.

Each photo carries an SHA-256 hash on storage. If you ever need to prove that a reading wasn't tampered with, the hash chain provides an independent audit trail.

EU-hosted, GDPR-aligned

Hosted in Frankfurt by default. We never send personal data to the U.S. unless your account is explicitly U.S.-based. PII is kept off any analytics pipelines we run.
