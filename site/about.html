About ECO-VPP.

A white-label platform built to dismantle the financial barriers of the energy transition — letting a 50-apartment block behave on the grid like a single 200 kW asset.

The problem

Most condominium residents can't afford a private solar + battery setup, and even when a building has rooftop PV, sharing it across apartments under EU's RED II Collective Self-Consumption rules is operationally hard. Existing VPP platforms target utilities and industrial sites — not the multi-unit residential market where 60% of Europeans live.

Our approach

Sub-€20 hardware

ESP32-S3 + ADE7913 metering frontend + 802.11ah HaLow modem. Cheap enough that the building covers it from the first month of shared solar.

Open-source bricks

Mainflux, FlexMeasures, GSY-e, Energy Web Origin/DID, AI-on-the-edge-device. We integrate, we don't reinvent.

Sharing-first

RED II priority: solar serves the community before it touches the wholesale grid. Surplus exits via the local P2P market, never as a passive feed-in tariff.

Resident-respecting

PII never leaves the building's database. Every node has a manual override pin. Privacy-preserving Merkle proofs replace raw consumption data on-chain.

FAQ

Is the platform really open-source?

Yes — Apache-2.0 license, full source on GitHub, no contributor-license-agreement required. White-label use (rebranding for your own community) is explicitly allowed.

Do residents need crypto wallets?

No. DIDs are managed by the building operator. Residents see euro amounts and bill credits — the on-chain settlement is invisible to them.

What if the cloud goes down?

Edge nodes buffer telemetry locally and replay on reconnect. The settlement service queues unsettled rows and resumes anchoring once the EW RPC is reachable. RTO target: 30 s. Verified by Chaos Mesh experiments shipped in the repo.

How do you keep PII off-chain?

Only DIDs (random opaque identifiers) and kWh deltas reach the chain. Names, addresses, and meter readings stay in the relational DB. Operators can serve GET /api/v1/proof/<tx> Merkle proofs for audit without exposing any PII.

Can I run only the parts I need?

Each service has its own image, deploy manifest, and Helm value flag (foo.enabled: false). Run only the webhook-receiver if you just need ingest. Run the full chain for a production pilot.

What about MiCA / EU crypto regulation?

The settlement layer produces a tradable Guarantee of Origin (GoO), which is the regulated artefact — not the underlying token. Settlement records form a MiCA-ready audit trail.
